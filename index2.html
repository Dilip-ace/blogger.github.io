Lakshadweep

Lakshadweep is a tropical archipelago of 36 atolls and coral reefs in the Laccadive Sea, off the coast of Kerala, India. Not all of the islands are inhabited, and only a few are open to visitors but permit is required. Kavaratti, one of the more developed islands. Kavaratti Aquarium, showcasing regional fish, shark and coral species. Kavaratti - Kavaratti is the capital of the Union Territory of Lakshadweep in India. Kavaratti is a census town as well as the name of the atoll upon which the town stands. It is well known for its pristine white sand beaches and calm lagoons, which makes it a popular tourist destination. Minicoy - Minicoy, locally known as Maliku is an island in Lakshadweep. Along with Viringili, it is on Maliku atoll, the southernmost atoll of Lakshadweep archipelago. Kadmat Island - Coral island & marine protected area with a white sandy beach, lagoon & village in an archipelago. Visit Lakshadweep and enjoy your vacation.
